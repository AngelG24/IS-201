Bonus Section

Okay, as promised, I'll be talking about my favorite talk from this last conference. When it came to this, it was super tough because they were all so incredible. I might mention bits and pieces of other talks, but let's get into it! My favorite was Elder Kearon's talk on God's Intent to Bring You Home. This one was a home run for me when I watched it in person, and I have probably either listened or rewatched it at least another five times since. The most significant point he alluded to is how Heavenly Father's plan is designed to bring all his children home, not to keep us out. He does not choose and pick who He wants to return to Him and who He doesn't; "He employs every possible measure to bring you back." Pausing here, these early words spoken by Him struck me hard. It caused me to reflect on my first semesters at BYU because they were a pain when it came to academics. I didn't perform poorly but didn't perform to the standard or passion for the material I was accustomed to. I was doing all the little things and asking Heavenly Father for help, but I received no answer for months. It felt like He was trying to drive me away from the church, and at some point, I even convinced myself of that. Fast forward to now, I'm no longer in that state of mind. My faith is on a much stronger foundation than ever before, and I have a better idea of the direction I want to take in life and the eternities. Like me, Heavenly provides all the resources to all His children that we need to succeed in this life and return to Him. In some circumstances, those resources may be harder to recognize, but they are always there because He loves us. He has no reason to put roadblocks before us that don't need to be there.

Elder Kearon then continued his message by connecting it with the Atonement. Because of his atoning sacrifice, "every roadblock of physical and spiritual death that would separate us from our eternal home" is removed. ‚ÄúFor I came from heaven, not to do mine own will, but the will of him that sent me. (John 6:38)" Heavenly Father sent down His Only Begotten Son to partake of the bitter cup and make it possible for all His children to one day return. I then loved how he referred to the parable of the Lost Sheep. Like Heavenly Father, our Savior will search for all His lost sheep until He finds them because He wants us to return to Heavenly Father's kingdom one day. His infinite Atonement covers all of the Father's children; His arm is extended to everyone. "I will not leave you comfortless: I will come to you. (John 14:18)"
